My Top 5 Songs That Are Stuck in My Brain

By De Leon Kenxie Jermaine S. February 16, 2026

Have you got one of those songs where it just gets stuck in your brain for a few months or even years? Or even days where you just walk and randomly hum it out? Well, these 5 songs have happened to me, and here they are.

1. Lorien Testard - Une vie à t'aimer

Genre: Orchestral / Soundtrack

Lorien Testard has created one of the most beloved soundtracks in today’s gaming industry. This 11-minute track shifts genres seamlessly, keeping listeners engaged from start to finish. Even without understanding the lyrics, the emotion feels like witnessing a grieving husband and wife in conflict. The music was featured during an important boss battle in Clair Obscur: Expedition 33, a title that earned nine awards. Interestingly, the directors discovered Testard through a random Russian music website that is no longer available today.

2. Kpop Demon Hunters - How It’s Done

Genre: K-Pop / Trap

This song blends trap and pop with explosive energy. Created by EJAE, one of the main vocalists in the hit movie Kpop Demon Hunters, it opens the film with an unforgettable punch. EJAE revealed that nearly 50 demos were made because the team wanted the opening track to be absolutely perfect.

3. Porter Robinson - Cheerleader

Genre: Pop-Rock / Electronic

Part of Porter Robinson’s third album, this track surprised fans by leaning into a pop-rock style rather than his usual EDM sound. He also showcased more of his vocals, despite previously saying he felt uncomfortable singing. The song expresses gratitude toward his fans, acknowledging that without them, he wouldn’t be where he is today.

4. Paramore - Still Into You

Genre: Pop-Rock / Alternative

Paramore formed before I was even born, but I discovered them through a game I played. “Still Into You” is a catchy pop-rock track filled with vibrant guitar melodies. The lyrics celebrate deep love and devotion, although real-life events later revealed that the person cheated at the singer.

5. Dareharu - Karma

Genre: Korean Rock

This Korean rock track may sound upbeat, but its lyrics explore betrayal and emotional pain from being backstabbed by close friends. Even after two years, it remains stuck in my head. Sadly, Dareharu disbanded after releasing only five songs following the passing of their songwriter, and their final song was dedicated to him. Due to them only having 5 songs they don't have an album or have shown their faces in publoc.
