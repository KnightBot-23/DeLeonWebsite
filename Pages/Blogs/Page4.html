My Top 5 Songs That Are Stuck in My Brain

By De Leon Kenxie Jermaine S. February 16, 2026

1. Lorien Testard - Une vie à t'aimer

Genre: Orchestral / Soundtrack

Lorien Testard has created one of the most beloved soundtracks in today’s gaming industry. This 11-minute track shifts genres seamlessly, keeping listeners engaged from start to finish. Even without understanding the lyrics, the emotion feels like witnessing a grieving husband and wife in conflict.

2. Kpop Demon Hunters - How It’s Done

Genre: K-Pop / Trap

This song blends trap and pop with explosive energy. Created by EJAE, one of the main vocalists in the hit movie Kpop Demon Hunters, it opens the film with an unforgettable punch. EJAE revealed that nearly 50 demos were made because the team wanted the opening track to be absolutely perfect.

3. Porter Robinson - Cheerleader

Genre: Pop-Rock / Electronic

Part of Porter Robinson’s third album, this track surprised fans by leaning into a pop-rock style rather than his usual EDM sound. He also showcased more of his vocals, despite previously saying he felt uncomfortable singing. The song expresses gratitude toward his fans.

4. Paramore - Still Into You

Genre: Pop-Rock / Alternative

Paramore formed before I was even born, but I discovered them through a game I played. “Still Into You” is a catchy pop-rock track filled with vibrant guitar melodies. The lyrics celebrate deep love and devotion, although real-life events later added more context.

5. Dareharu - Karma

Genre: Pop Rock / Alternative

This Korean rock track explores betrayal and emotional pain from being backstabbed by close friends. Even after two years, it remains stuck in my head. Sadly, Dareharu disbanded after releasing only five songs following the passing of their songwriter.
